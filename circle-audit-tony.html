Why now

Circle Eclipse changed what the platform can do

On 16 June, Circle announced five things at once: an AI partner built into the platform, a unified inbox, rebuilt courses, a member marketplace called Discover, and a studio service for very large creators. The rollout reaches every customer by 1 August.

Most of it is included on your plan at no extra cost. Discover in particular is a marketplace Circle is routing more than 100,000 people a month into — and joining it is a switch, not a project.

But Eclipse is only half the reason. Your plan is a legacy Enterprise tier Circle no longer sells, and it carries features most Circle customers cannot access at all: AI Agents, AI workflows, unlimited workflows, a sandbox environment, custom profile fields, a dedicated success manager at Circle.

My honest read, after a few years working inside your account, is that a good portion of that has never been turned on. Not through neglect — because the weekly work always comes first. That is exactly why this needs to sit outside the weekly work.
